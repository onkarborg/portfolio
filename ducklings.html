Ducklings

To help you organize the Memories of your baby.

Project Overview

The Problem: Parents are motivated andexcited to document and share the growth of their children. The depth ofdetails and variety of documentation is limitless and the fear of missing outmakes them generate a large amount. This leaves them with the daunting task toorganize them. In addition, privacy concern plays a vital role in theirhesitation to use existing technologies. We want to explore solutions toaddress these pain points so documentation and sharing of their child’s growthis secure, organized and doesn’t take them away from enjoying the present withtheir children. 

Our Solution: We conducted several interviews with a parents, asking them the challenges faced while taking care of their baby. Coming up with a list of requirements, we decided against creating a brand new appliction for parents which satisfy their needs. Instead, we decided to create an add-on to google drive which is a famous platform for storing documents for many users. This add-on will sync and update all documents related to the baby along with provide options for other contibutors to view or add more documents.

Our Process

User Interviews

My team started the project by conducting user interviews. We talked to parents about the problems faced by them during the initial 2 years after the birth of their baby. The problems we focused were the ones related to documentations or preserving of photographs or notes or schedules or any special events related to their baby. We found that most parents simply gave up on documenting their memories in fear of not giving their babies enough attention. As understandable as the problem was, we set out to find a solution to this dilemma.

Some of the most important requirements mentioned by the parents were:

  • Comprehensive Privacy Settings
  • Multiple Views of Memories
  • Preset Options 
  • Accessible over various platforms

Interpretion

After gathering a huge amount of data from the users, we sat down to interpret the data and brainstorm a few solutions. We first wanted to get a better grip of the problem space. In order to understand the requirements of our users and try to find solutions for their problems, we wanted to find how the problems were connected and how we could reach the core issues rather than the surface problems mentioned by our users. We constructed an affinity diagram and cognitive maps parallely to try to solve the issues.

Prototyping

We decided to design a plugin or an add-on to an existing storage platform. As a team, we decided it wasn't reasonable to ask the users to use one more app when there was such a constraint on time and attention they could spare. Hence, we imagined our solution to be an auto-sync add-on to an existing storage platform such as Google Drive. Another reason we agreed with a plugin instead of a full blown app was that Google already uses an add-on called Photos which auto-syncs images from the phone to the Drive. Our plugin would be able to synchronize images, videos, notes, voice recordings, events, and schedules. We also wanted to provide affordance to external users to contribute to the documentation. Our plugin would also create videos, timelines, visualizations of the memories of the amusement of viewers.

How our solution address the users requirements

  • Comprehensive Privacy Settings: all the security Google provides its users along with full control to both the parents on what should be a final addition to the documentation, on who can view the content and who can add to the content.
  • Multiple Views of Memories: This feature allows the viewers to view the memories using keywords, chronological order or by events as set by the parents.
  • Preset Options:  The design allows the user to present the memories and sharing them using one-click creation
  • Accessible over various platforms: A plugin to Google Drive allows the user to use our system on the phone app or via the website.

We proceeded to create a low-fidelity prototype of our system. We focused mainly on the desktop website as most of the screen on the mobile interface were redundant. Usability Testing

Before proceeding to the next stage of creating a high-fidelity prototype, we conducted Cognitive Walkthroughs and Heauristic Evaluation of our design to make a more usable and intuitive design.

Cognitive Walkthrough: We came up with many findings while conducting a walkthrough.

  • Needs consistent Iconography
  • adding /editing contributors option isn’t very visible
  • Lacks visual cues for task completion or failure

Heuristic Evaluation: Comparing our design againt the heuristics of design revealed many pit-falls in our design. such as:

  • Misleading and Unclear labels on Menu items:
  • On the customize page, there is no indication as to the current process they are in.
  • The labelling of the group icon is inconsistent.
  • There is no flexibility to directly to add memories to child’s file.
  • Using the eye icon to view the picture is rather difficult
